25 Cat-Safe Houseplants (and 10 Toxic Ones to Avoid)

✍️ Dr. Sarah Mitchell, DVM 📅 June 1, 2026 ⏱️ 10 min read
🪴

Houseplants add life and beauty to your home, but many common plants are toxic to cats. With cats' natural curiosity and tendency to chew on greenery, knowing which plants are safe is essential for every cat owner.

We consulted with veterinary toxicologists and the ASPCA Animal Poison Control database to compile this comprehensive guide.

✅ 25 Cat-Safe Houseplants

These plants have been confirmed safe by the ASPCA and veterinary toxicologists. While "safe" means non-toxic, excessive consumption of any plant can cause mild stomach upset in cats.

#Plant NameNotes
1Spider Plant (Chlorophytum comosum)Very popular, mildly hallucinogenic to cats (they love it) but non-toxic
2Boston Fern (Nephrolepis exaltata)Safe and great for humidity; keep soil moist
3Areca Palm (Dypsis lutescens)Beautiful tropical plant; safe for all pets
4Bamboo Palm (Chamaedorea seifrizii)Air-purifying and cat-safe
5African Violet (Saintpaulia)Pretty flowering plant; completely safe
6Calathea / Prayer PlantBeautiful patterns; non-toxic to cats and dogs
7PeperomiaOver 1,000 varieties, all non-toxic
8Orchid (Phalaenopsis)Common orchids are safe; exotic species may vary
9HaworthiaSafe succulent unlike its cousin aloe vera
10Catnip (Nepeta cataria)Safe and cats love it! Grows easily indoors
11Cat Grass / WheatgrassDesigned for cats; helps digestion and hairballs
12BromeliadsTropical and colorful; safe for cats
13Polka Dot Plant (Hypoestes)Colorful, fun, and safe
14Rattlesnake Plant (Calathea lancifolia)Striking appearance; non-toxic
15Swedish Ivy (Plectranthus australis)Easy to grow trailing plant; safe
16Lemon Balm (Melissa officinalis)Herbal plant; safe and cats enjoy the smell
17RosemaryCulinary herb; non-toxic to cats
18ThymeAnother safe culinary herb
19ValerianHas similar effects to catnip on some cats; safe
20Snap Dragon (Antirrhinum)Beautiful flowering plant; non-toxic
21Wax Plant (Hoya)Easy-care trailing plant; safe for cats
22Friendship Plant (Pilea involucrata)Compact and safe
23Air Plants (Tillandsia)No soil needed; completely cat-safe

25Ponytail Palm (Beaucarnea recurvata)Drought-tolerant and cat-safe
💡 Cat Grass Tip: Growing cat grass or catnip indoors gives your cat a safe "chew toy" that may actually reduce interest in your other plants.

❌ 10 Toxic Plants — Keep Away from Cats

These plants are toxic to cats and should be kept completely out of reach, or ideally, removed from your home entirely if you have cats:

#PlantToxicity LevelSymptoms
1Lilies (all species)HIGHLY TOXICKidney failure — even small amounts can be fatal. Emergency vet immediately.
2Sago PalmHIGHLY TOXICLiver failure, seizures — extremely dangerous
3OleanderHIGHLY TOXICHeart arrhythmia, can be fatal
4Azalea / RhododendronHIGHLY TOXICVomiting, tremors, coma
5Daffodil / NarcissusTOXICVomiting, diarrhea, tremors (bulbs most toxic)
6Pothos / Devil's IvyTOXICOral irritation, vomiting, difficulty swallowing
7Peace LilyTOXICOral swelling, vomiting, kidney issues in large amounts
8Aloe VeraTOXICVomiting, diarrhea, lethargy
9Snake Plant / Mother-in-Law's TongueMILDLY TOXICNausea, vomiting if ingested in quantity
10ZZ Plant (Zamioculcas)MILDLY TOXICSkin and oral irritation
🚨 Lilies are a cat emergency: True lilies (Tiger lily, Easter lily, Asiatic lily, Daylily) are extremely dangerous. If your cat chews on any part of a lily — even licking pollen off their fur — call your vet or ASPCA Animal Poison Control (888-426-4435) immediately. Kidney failure can occur within 36–72 hours without treatment.

What to Do If Your Cat Eats a Toxic Plant

  1. Identify the plant as specifically as possible (take a photo)
  2. Call your veterinarian or ASPCA Animal Poison Control: 888-426-4435 (24/7)
  3. Do NOT induce vomiting unless specifically instructed
  4. Bring a sample of the plant to the vet if possible

Tips for Plant-Proofing Your Home

  • Place plants on high shelves or in rooms your cat can't access
  • Use hanging planters to keep plants off surfaces
  • Try citrus sprays or aluminum foil around plant bases — most cats dislike these
  • Grow cat grass or catnip to redirect your cat's plant-chewing instincts
  • Consider artificial plants for your favorite toxic species
